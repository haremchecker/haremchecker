About This Site

This site aims to be a quick tool to check whether an anime/manga/novel is what the owner terms a "true" harem, or a "psuedo" harem. The owner passes absolutely no judgment on an individual's preferences. This is simply a tool to help an individual find, or avoid, specific titles.

If you notice any errors, or would like to suggest a title not currently included, please contact the owner.

Many works are tagged with harem (or sometimes not), especially on anime and manga trackers, but not all of them end up being an actual harem. Some may look like a harem based on the promotional art but not be, and some may not look like a harem but are. So the owner differentiates between the two.

  • A pseudo-harem is a work tagged as a harem, that may have harem-like elements. This is usually, but not limited to, multiple potential love interests for the protagonist. However, in the end, despite multiple options, the protagonist only ends up with a single person. Also a possibility is an ending where the protagonist ends up with nobody at all. An example of a pseudo-harem is DanMachi.
  • A true harem is when the protagonist ends up in a romantic/sexual relationship with some, or all, of the potential love interests. For the sake of brevity, any protagonist with more than one concurrent partner is listed in this category. An example of a true harem is High School DxD.
  • A reverse harem is when the protagonist is female, and the love interests are male, (although there might occasionally be females among the group vying for the protagonist's affections). It is incredibly rare for a reverse harem to end with polyamory, so they have their own category. An example of a reverse harem is My Next Life as a Villainess: All Roads Lead to Doom.

A note on spoilers: All titles will be tagged based on the source material wherever possible. This means that anime watchers may encounter a title with a status other than what they were expecting. Please be aware of this.
